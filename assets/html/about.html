This dashboard is a tool that shows the prevalence and patterns of mental health illness as well as the related health care service based on county-level data among the United States. It also compares the public interests in mental health with the data from Google Search Trends. Our team developed this resource to track services that are critically important to public health and a well-functioning health care system. Researchers, policymakers, and journalists can use this data to:
  • Monitor the prevalence and compare the patterns of mental health illness in U.S. population by age, race, and regions
  • Compare the geographical distribution of mental health service use
  • Pinpoint the effects of national and state level public health policies and initiatives on health care use
  • Observe the impact of natural phenomena, such as the COVID-19 pandemic, on the public interest in mental health related affairs
    • Primarily, the dashboard allows users to track patterns of mental health illness. This standardized measure enables comparisons of the volume of cases reported and health services used across different populations. This dashboard presents data at a county level. Users can filter and stratify the data by illness category and detailed age group, race, state, and county.
    Data Source
    The data underlying this dashboard is compiled from administrative health care claims datasets:
  • National Survey on Drug Use and Health (NSDUH)
  • Mental Health America
  • Health Resources & Services Administration
  • Google Trends
    • Note that the data is not available for all states. The dashboard will be updated periodically as new data becomes available.
